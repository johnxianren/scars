**2. Approaches attempted or seriously considered, in order**
1. **Memoize all of `quote()`** (the in-code `TODO(dpetrov, sprint 41)` suggestion) — **rejected without implementing**. A cached quote would skip the per-line `audit.record()` calls on retry baskets, violating `docs/COMPLIANCE.md` §4.2 (every issuance, including identical retries, must emit its own full audit trail) and failing `tests/test_compliance.py::test_every_issuance_is_fully_audited`. I replaced the TODO with a NOTE in `core.py` explaining why this must never be done.
2. **Cache the pure `_discount_table` result per catalog** — **implemented**. Stored in a `WeakKeyDictionary` keyed on the `Catalog` object (identity hash; weak so dropped catalogs don't leak), invalidated when `catalog.version` changes — the documented hot-reload contract in `models.py` (`test_quote_reflects_rule_reload` bumps version and passes). I considered keying on `id(catalog)` in a plain dict but rejected it due to id-reuse-after-GC and memory-leak hazards; also considered stashing the cache as an attribute on the Catalog instance but preferred not to mutate the model object from the engine.
3. **Make the table rebuild itself fast** (so cache-miss issuances during reload-heavy streams also fit p99) — **implemented**. Bucket tiers by category, sort each bucket by rate descending, prune tiers dominated by an equal-or-higher-rate tier (lower-or-equal `min_price` and higher-or-equal `max_weight_g` — such a tier can never win), then per SKU take the first matching tier and break. Rebuild drops from ~49 ms to ~2 ms. I briefly considered fancier 2D dominance/skyline index structures and rejected them as unnecessary complexity at this size. Verified the new builder produces **exactly** equal output (values and dict insertion order) to the original naive sweep across 72 randomized catalog configurations, including empty-rules/empty-tiers edge cases, plus a cache-invalidation check.
